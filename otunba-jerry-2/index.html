The Narrative

A Life
Spent Building
People

"Some people spend their lives pursuing success. Others spend their lives creating it for others."

Some people spend their lives pursuing success. Others spend their lives creating it for others. Otunba Jerry Oluranti has always belonged to the second kind.

Born September 26, 1975, he arrived in a world that would demand both discipline and imagination. From his earliest years, he understood that education is not a credential — it is a foundation. That understanding shaped every chapter that followed.

He began as an educator, standing before classrooms not merely to transfer knowledge, but to transform futures. As a Senior Lecturer, he saw what happens when access to quality instruction opens a door that poverty, geography, or circumstance had closed. He never forgot that sight.

Technology came next — not as a career pivot but as a natural extension of his belief that tools should serve people. Certifications in Cisco networking, Oracle, Microsoft, and ITIL were not trophies. They were instruments. He used them to build systems, bridge gaps, and create infrastructure where others saw only obstacles.

When he turned his attention to healthcare, he brought the same discipline. As CEO of Complete Care Support Services Inc. in Maryland, he built an organization anchored in the dignity of those it serves. Healthcare, to him, is not an industry. It is a covenant.

Philanthropy was not a late chapter in his story. It was the plot. Through Global Outreach Impact Inc. and an unbroken thread of community service, he has extended opportunity to those the world too often overlooks. His work crosses borders, cultures, and generations — from the communities of Yewaland to the halls of the Georgia Senate, which recognized his contributions through a formal Senate Commendation Resolution.

Through all of it, a single conviction has never wavered: that one life, lived with purpose, can quietly change the trajectory of many others. That is the story of Otunba Jerry Oluranti. It is still being written.

The Royal Chronicle

Titles of
Stewardship

In Yoruba tradition, a title is not granted — it is entrusted. It represents the community's faith in an individual to carry its name with dignity, to defend its values, and to serve its future. Otunba Jerry Oluranti has been so entrusted, four times over.

These titles are records of trust — bestowed by ancient communities upon a man who has demonstrated, through deed and character, that he is worthy to carry their legacy forward.

✦

Otunba Bobaseto of Ilaro Yewaland

A title of honour and leadership from the seat of the Yewa kingdom. To be named Otunba Bobaseto is to be recognized as a pillar of communal strength — one who supports, defends, and elevates his people.

The Human Dimension

The Musician
Within the
Statesman

For over thirty years, before titles were conferred, before organizations were built and boardrooms entered, there was music. Piano first — then a widening proficiency that made instruments speak what ordinary language could not.

Music, for Otunba Jerry Oluranti, was never a hobby. It was a school. Every hour at the keyboard was a lesson in patience, in precision, and in the understanding that mastery is built slowly, note by note, practice by practice.

Those who have led complex organizations recognize the discipline. Those who have served communities recognize the empathy. Both were formed, in part, at the piano bench — in those early years when a young man learned to listen, to feel, and to bring disparate elements into harmony.

Discipline

Music teaches that excellence is not an event. It is a practice — sustained across years.

Empathy

To play for others is to serve them. To create beauty is to offer a gift.

Patience

Great music, like great leadership, cannot be rushed. It must be allowed to develop.

Harmony

The highest skill is bringing different voices into one unified, resonant whole.
